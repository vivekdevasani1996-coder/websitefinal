Goods & Service Tax

Expert GST guidance, registration, filing, audits, and dispute resolution for seamless tax compliance.

About GST
At Avi & Co, we provide expert guidance on GST (Goods and Services Tax), a comprehensive indirect tax that replaced multiple taxes such as VAT, excise duty, and service tax. GST is applied to the supply of goods and services at every stage of production and distribution. Its primary goal is to simplify the tax system, eliminate tax cascades, and enable smooth credit flow across the supply chain. As a destination-based tax, it is levied where the goods or services are ultimately consumed.

Benefits of GST
Unified Tax System
Replaces multiple indirect taxes with a single tax, streamlining the entire tax system.
Full Transparency
Enables real-time tracking of transactions and tax payments for complete transparency.
Input Tax Credits
Businesses can claim input tax credits for taxes paid on inputs, reducing overall tax burden.
Seamless Interstate Trade
Reduces barriers to interstate trade by eliminating cascading taxes.
Digital Compliance
Online registration, filing, and payment systems simplify compliance and reduce costs.
Consumer Benefits
Consumers benefit from clearer tax-inclusive pricing due to GST's transparency.

FAQs — Goods & Service Tax
What is GST?
GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a single tax on the supply of goods and services, replacing multiple indirect taxes.
Who needs to register for GST?
Businesses with a turnover above a specified threshold and those engaged in inter-state supply or e-commerce need to register.
What are the GST tax rates?
GST rates vary by product and service, generally classified into standard rates (e.g., 5%, 12%, 18%, 28%) and special rates.
How does GST benefits businesses?
It simplifies the tax structure, eliminates tax-on-tax, and allows input tax credits, reducing the overall tax burden.
What is an input tax credit (ITC)?
ITC allows businesses to claim credit for taxes paid on inputs used in their business, offsetting their GST liability.
Can consumers claim a refund on GST?
Generally, consumers cannot directly claim a refund for GST paid. However, businesses may claim refunds for excess tax paid, which could, in turn, lower the cost to consumers.
How often do businesses need to file GST returns?
Businesses need to file GST returns periodically, typically monthly or quarterly (depending on turnover), with the main returns being GSTR-1 (sales) and GSTR-3B (summary return).
What happens if businesses fail to comply with GST rules?
Non-compliance with GST rules can lead to penalties, fines, and legal consequences. It can also affect a business’s ability to claim input tax credits and negatively impact their reputation.
How can businesses claim input tax credits (ITC)?
Businesses can claim input tax credits on taxes paid for goods and services used in their operations. To do this, they must ensure that the purchases are GST-compliant and properly recorded in their returns.
Do businesses have to charge GST on all goods and services?
No, GST is applicable based on the type of goods or services. Some items may be exempt or subject to lower tax rates, while others may fall under standard rates.

What is a Society?
A society is an association of individuals united for a common purpose, typically formed for charitable, cultural, religious, or educational purposes.
How is a society different from a Trust?
Unlike a Trust, which is managed by trustees, a society is governed by a managing committee elected by its members.
How is a society registered?
A society is registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, by submitting the Memorandum of Association and Rules & Regulations.
What are the benefits of forming a society?
Societies enjoy legal recognition, can own property, and are eligible for certain tax exemptions.
Who can form a society?
At least seven people are required to form a society, and they must agree to work together for a common goal.
What are the compliance requirements for a society?
Societies must maintain records, hold annual general meetings, file annual returns, and adhere to their own rules and regulations.
How are the funds of a society managed?
Funds are managed by the managing committee and must be used exclusively for the society’s objectives.
Can a society be dissolved?
Yes, a society can be dissolved as per its rules or by a court order, and its assets are transferred to another society with similar objectives.
What are the tax implications for a society?
Societies may qualify for tax exemptions if they are formed for charitable purposes and meet the conditions laid out by the Income Tax Act.
Can a society enagage in business activites?
A society can engage in business activities, provided the profits are used for the society’s objectives and not distributed among members.

FAQs — Private Limited Company
What is a Private Limited Company?
A private limited company is a type of business entity that is privately held and offers limited liability protection to its shareholders.
How do I register a Private Limited Company?
You can register through the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) portal by submitting necessary documentation including name approval, MOA, AOA, and director identity proofs. We handle the entire process for you.
What are the minimum requirements?
Minimum 2 directors, 2 shareholders, a registered office, and capital (no minimum capital mandate in India as of now).
Can a Private Limited Company have foreign shareholders?
Yes, foreign individuals or companies can hold shares subject to FDI rules and regulations.
What are the benefits of Private Limited Company?
Key benefits include limited liability protection, separate legal entity status, easier capital raising, and enhanced credibility.
How are the profits of a Private Limited Company taxed?
The company’s profits are taxed at the corporate tax rate, and dividends distributed to shareholders may also be subject to dividend distribution tax.
What are the compliance requirements for a Private Limited Company?
Annual compliance includes filing financial statements, annual returns, conducting board meetings, and maintaining statutory records.
What is the difference between a Private Limited Company and a Public Limited Company?
A private limited company restricts the transfer of its shares and limits the number of shareholders, whereas a public limited company can offer its shares to the public.
How can I dissolve a Private Limited Company?
Dissolution involves settling any outstanding debts, distributing remaining assets to shareholders, and filing the necessary documents with the Registrar of Companies for official closure.

The Income-tax Act, 2025 (replacing the 61-year-old 1961 Act) comes into force on 1 April 2026. Updated ITR utilities for AY 2026-27 under the new regime are now available on the e-filing portal. Taxpayers are encouraged to download updated offline utilities for ITR-1 through ITR-7.

Budget 2026
Revised ITR Filing Deadlines & Extended Revision Window — Budget 2026
Budget 2026 proposes staggered ITR deadlines: 31 July for individuals (ITR-1/ITR-2) and 31 August for non-audit business cases. Revised ITR deadline extended to 31 March of the following year (with a nominal fee). Depositories may also accept Form 15G/15H from investors holding multiple securities.

CBDT Notifies Income-tax Rules 2026 — Comprehensive Procedural Framework
CBDT Notification No. 22/2026 notifies the Income-tax Rules, 2026 under Section 533 of the IT Act, 2025, effective 1 April 2026. Covers valuation of assets, non-resident taxation, digital economy thresholds, salary perquisite treatment, and zero coupon bond procedures. New Forms 1–4 prescribed in Appendix III.

CBDT Issues UIN Framework for Form 121 Declarations — Effective 1 April 2026
CBDT Notification No. 01/CPC(TDS)/2026 mandates a new 26-character Unique Identification Number (UIN) system for every Form 121 declaration. Payers must generate and report UINs for each declaration — including manually received ones — starting 1 April 2026.

Corrigendum: "First Amendment Rules 2026" Title Correction via G.S.R. 189(E)
CBDT issued a corrigendum correcting the title of G.S.R. 158(E) dated 5 March 2026. Rules are now formally designated as the "Income-tax (First Amendment) Rules, 2026" — establishing the sequential numbering convention for all 2026 amendments.

31 Mar 2026
Director KYC Frequency Changed to Once in 3 Years — Effective Today
MCA replaced the annual DIR-3 KYC filing with a simpler KYC intimation once every three years [Gazette Notification G.S.R. 943(E) dated 31 December 2025]. Directors who completed KYC to date: next KYC due by 30 June 2028. Directors who have not yet submitted KYC must reactivate DIN by today (31 March 2026).

MCA Operationalises 3 New Regional Directorates & 6 New ROCs from 1 Jan 2026
Three new Regional Directorates (Chandigarh, Navi Mumbai, Bengaluru) and six new ROCs (Delhi I & II, Mumbai, Kolkata, Noida, Nagpur, Chandigarh) were operationalised from 1 January 2026. Companies in affected jurisdictions have been remapped to the appropriate new offices.
